==You are already recording==
In some ways, you may already be recording your history.

* Email accounts contain a timeline of your conversations and relationships with friends, acquaintances, family, and the outside world.
* Photo albums can contain timestamps, geolocations, and of course, other human beings. This forms a deep history of your activities, travels, and friendships.

For these things, you need to focus on [[Preservation]], and on [[Reconstruction]] of the missing pieces.

But there are a few other things which you can do to record a more in-depth picture of your life.

==Recording Your Life==
[[https://openpaths.cc/ | Open Paths]] - a great location tool which allows you to export and control all your data
[[http://blog.brianhouse.net/post/7359294387 | See how someone turned OpenPaths into a video of their commute]]
[[http://jsomers.net/blog/jimbopad |JimboPad]] - record your entire writing process

==Three Pillars==
For many people, a record of locations, an extensive photo collection (taken with a phone or modern camera), and an email archive, form a deep history of their lives. 

These can be considered the three pillars of life-recording.

Unfortunately, not many people think much about preserving these resources. Take a look at [[Preservation]].
